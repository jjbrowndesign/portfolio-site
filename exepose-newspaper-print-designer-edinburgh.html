Print Design

Exeposé newspaper

In 2016, I spent most of my summer redesigning the student newspaper, in my role of Editor. My redesign was inspired by research into other student newspapers, including the University of Cambridge’s Varsity and the University of York’s Nouse.

The feedback from readers was exactly as I’d hoped: more sophisticated, more modern, and more creative.

☟ Highlights of the project


Refreshing the brand

We chose a new logo (including seasonal variations!) and pulled the front page design closer to a mass-market newspaper, improving clarity and accessibility.

☟

A splash of colour

The ubiquitous grey boxes were some of the first elements to go, to be replaced by the clear section colour palettes and stylish fonts.

☟

Freedom to create

As part of the redesign process, I brought the arts and culture sections together into a more creative and vibrant feature section called ‘Exhibit’. The idea was to make it similar to a culture pullout in a national newspaper, and to encourage writers to cross-pollinate between these similar sections.

White space

It wasn't just the arts and culture section to get a redesign. In the Features sections, clunky bold headlines were thrown away, and a new appreciation for white space was added.
