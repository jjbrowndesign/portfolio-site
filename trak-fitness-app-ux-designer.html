Case study

Creating an inclusive product

An app for runners that uses progressive disclosure to satisfy beginners and experts alike. Allowing users to find the perfect running route.


1. User Personas

  • Conducting a competitor analysis
  • Interviewing potential users
  • Creating user personas

After collecting insights from potential users, I concluded the app should appeal to entry-level runners who want to find an enjoyable route locally but are uncomfortable with the seriousness or competitiveness of Strava.

However, I learnt it would also be valuable for serious runners who are looking for a challenging new route and want to assess the statistics in depth.

2. User Flow Diagrams

  • Writing Jobs To Be Done
  • Sketching User Flows
  • Finalising a User Flow Diagram

My ‘Jobs To Be Done’ list focused primarily on the options that were offered on the Plan a Route screen: distance/time, elevation, terrain type, and so on.

I focused on the Plan a Route functionality of the app as a priority, but also sketched out some social possibilities that could be further developed in future iterations of the app.

3. Prototype testing

  • Building a prototype on InVision
  • Conducting usability testing
  • Analysing the results

After some rapid Crazy 8s sketching, I drew a low-fidelity wireframe, and then developed a higher-fidelity version for prototype testing online. I wouldn’t always show so much detail in an early prototype, but in this case it felt as though the app had enough intricacies that it would benefit from showing more of the offerings.

4. Style guide

  • Producing a Style Guide
  • Constructing a brand identity
  • Designing imagery and icons

The idea of a gradient showing areas of high and low pace was very popular in the usability testing, so this became a key aspect of the app. Although a traffic light system might be more recognisable, I thought it would be likely to clash with the brand identity. Instead, I developed a teal-turquoise gradient that looked stylish and also felt suitably related to health and fitness.

5. Final product

The final product consisted of about 25-30 screens, plus various assets including icons and images. I also set up an interactive prototype to show how the screens all link together.
