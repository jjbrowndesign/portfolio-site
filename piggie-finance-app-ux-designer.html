Case study

Implementing a new brand

A fun and friendly app helping kids to manage their pocket money, and teaching them how to save. Applying a strong brand identity through UI.


1. Brand Guidelines

  • Finding some guiding principles
  • Designing a child-friendly brand
  • Settling on a friendly tone

I have a real passion for creating (and reading about) brand guidelines, so it was interesting to start by considering how the brand should look. From crafting some key principles to adopting a chatty tone of voice, this really solidified a basic concept before taking that to users for assessment.

2. Wireframes

  • Sketching Low Fidelity Wireframes
  • Gathering feedback on High Fidelity Wireframes
  • Ironing out a final design

I followed a similar process to previous projects here, but challenged myself to act rapidly and flexibly. To represent this, I’ve shown the iterations of a single screen below.

Although I gained useful feedback from fellow designers, it would - of course - be far more helpful to find some children to test these designs if I was to continue the project further. In particular, I think I need to consider the specific age range of the app in more detail.

3. Final product

The final product consisted of 13 screens, which represents a basic MVP.

My ‘Spending’ and ‘Account’ sections are still to follow.
