About me

What was my path to UX?

Let me take you back to 2017. Fidget spinners were trending, Despacito was in the charts, and people were asking Google “What is a hung parliament?”

Meanwhile, I’d started working for a small company in London. If you were a leading graduate employer like the Civil Service or Deloitte, we would conduct user research, testing your graduate website with university students.

Back then, it was my job to take the data and present it in clear and informative research reports, so you could make improvements to your site with the user firmly in mind.

Over the next three years, I’d become especially passionate about this user-led, data-driven decision-making. I started to realise that my love of design and tech, together with my research background, could come together in a job as a UX Designer.

So I dived into a year-long intensive bootcamp in User Experience and User Interface Design.

I haven’t looked back since!
