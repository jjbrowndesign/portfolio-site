Case study

Taking UX inspiration from physical objects

Looking back to the enchanting mixtapes of the 80s and the tactile iPod clickwheel to create a playlist app.


1. Finding inspiration

  • Discovering a gap in the market
  • Researching UI patterns
  • Looking for inspiration

My app concept revolved around the idea of ‘mixing’ a playlist in a very interactive, fun and personalised way. Eventually, the idea of ‘creating a mix’ was born, inspired by the mixtapes of the 80s. I wanted to add some dynamism to my Now Playing page, and I remembered the spinning ‘rotary wheel’ of the old BBC iPlayer Radio app, which I always found pleasing to use. This was no doubt inspired itself by the much-loved iPod clickwheel.

2. Low-Fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity wireframes were quite simple to pull together, as I was already very aware of the common UI elements in a music app.

3. High-Fidelity Wireframes

The higher-fidelity digital wireframes took a bit longer to perfect, but came out well.

4. Moodboard

  • Producing a moodboard
  • Working on text and typography
  • Finalising a colour scheme

A recurring part of my moodboard was an eye-catching colour scheme. As I was targeting the app at young people, I knew there was more flexibility in choosing risky accent colours. Pink was a common feature, partly due to the 80s neon-inspired designs I’d picked out. ‘Living Coral’ was the Pantone Colour of the Year last year, thanks in part to its use by modern, forward-thinking brands (like Monzo bank’s instantly recognisable credit card), so a bold pink seemed like a good choice.

5. Final product

The final product consisted of about eight key screens, plus various assets including icons and images. I also set up an interactive prototype to demonstrate how the screens all link together.
